Glossier mini case study

Tools used : Sketch | After Effects | Adobe Photoshop | Adobe Illustrator
Project duration : 5 hours

Glossier is a beauty company that has amassed a cult following amongst Millennials and Gen Z’ers through their strong use of highlighting individualism. I sought to re-create an easy landing page and a new feature to aid consumers in finding their perfect shade of makeup.

I began by conducting in person research at multiple branches of the successful beauty empire Sephora. I surveyed consumers with 4 options as to what kept coming back to the store.
1. Full Makeup Display
2. The option to try it on
3. Customer service
4. Other

The number one poll went to "the option to try it on”. In a close second was a "full makeup display / with the option to view the product”. Thirdly the most popular opinion given for the “other” category was that consumers could shop at one store for multiple brands.

Since glossier conducts the majority of its sales through e-commerce, the option "to try it on” was a little trickier. I decided to take the second favorite approach of " fully displaying their products", and do it in an amusing way. Upon clicking on an item, the item then animates and an option to "try it on" appears. The try it on page leads to a form where one would apply to receive free samples.

On the right side of the screen there is a animating happy face sticker. The sticker is a re-interpretation of a sticker set I received from Glossier. It is used as a wildcard button. It could be used as an option to enter and win free products! An idea that adds some spontaneity to the page.

The tempting display of products is only a first taste. Users enter the shop by clicking the animating “G” glossier logo.

After reading through various online articles, the leading united hesitance towards purchasing online beauty products linked to the fear of receiving shades that didn't match ones skin tone. This page is devoted to finding a consumers perfect shade for all Glossier products. Ushering in the ability to "try on" a product without physical interaction.

One selects their skin tone and chooses a category of product. A section of products appear in the lowest box with an option to scroll for more. Once a product is chosen, an interactive video of a model with the chosen user's shade is shown applying the product. A video representation is able to show a better representation of depth as opposed to a classic flat photo reference.
