Misfits mini case study

Tools used : Sketch | After Effects | Adobe Photoshop | Adobe Illustrator
Project duration : 5 hours

Misfits is an online subscription box company that supplies rescued food from being unnecessarily thrown away. Upon researching more about the company, I came across common complaints in which consumers resulted in throwing away produce that were foreign to them. I quickly decided that perhaps the creation of a recipe blog would help consumers gain familiarity with all sold produce. I discovered that there was already a recipe blog created, but I felt the blog to be bland. Below are new interpretations I made in the time allotted.

The decision to create a mobile version was decided based on multiple online studies that proved consumers were increasingly looking to mobile to do their online grocery shopping. According to a 2019 CNBC article, Instacart CEO Apoorva Mehta stated that “... pretty much every major grocer in North America has chosen Instacart as their partner.”
[ Instacart is a popular app that delivers groceries from your local market to your door. ]



Screen 1 Features an orange background, which immediately distinguishes itself from Misfits' main website. Oranges and reds were chosen according to their ability to increase appetite. Utilizing colour theory has been used in all major fast food giants such as McDonald's, KFC , Burger King etc.

Screen 2 According to an article on Quicksprout, followers lose interest when content isn't routinely being supplied. A blinking sticker, that mimics that of a produce sticker keeps readers coming back by assuring them there will be daily content.

Screen 3 Enlarged fonts and colorful highlighted tabs help users easily navigate pages they would like to visit.



Among the few posts on Misfits' official blog, I came across an article titled "What the Heck do I do with broccoli?". I found the phrase "What the heck" to be an especially engrossing tagline ! Capitalizing and creating a series of recipes around this tagline would be a perfect and fun way to introduce unfamiliar produce to consumers. Also, the white background is easier on the eyes when paired in contrast to the colorful illustrations.

The above illustrations were intentionally drawn with a multitude of layers. A multidimensional image creates the illusion of richness. It helps to introduce Misfits' produce as bountiful, rich, and appetizing.
